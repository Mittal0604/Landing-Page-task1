Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the southern bank of the river Yamuna in the Indian city of Agra, was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar, a minaret that forms part of the Qutub complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Mehrauli area of Delhi, is one of the most iconic monuments of India, known for its architectural magnificence.

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India is an arch-monument built in the early twentieth century in the city of Mumbai, commemorating the landing of King George V and Queen Mary in 1911.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi in India that served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors. It is known for its massive enclosing walls of red sandstone.
